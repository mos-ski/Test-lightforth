# Lightforth — Competitive Battlecards

## How to Use This Document

Lead with Lightforth's strengths, not just a competitor's weaknesses.

**Framework:**
1. Acknowledge what they're already using
2. Pivot to our differentiation
3. Ask a qualifying question
4. Share a proof point

---

## vs. Teal

### Their Pitch
"Free resume builder and job application tracker."

### Their Strengths
- Free tier, huge user base
- Good application tracker / kanban board
- Browser extension for saving jobs

### Their Weaknesses
- **No Auto-Apply** — you still manually submit every application
- **No live interview coaching** — nothing during the actual interview
- **Resume tailoring is manual** — you edit it yourself, not AI-generated per job

### Our Counter
"Teal is a great tracker. But tracking jobs isn't the hard part — getting through the ATS and then not freezing in the actual interview is. That's the part Teal doesn't touch."

### Key Questions
- "How many hours a week are you spending manually tailoring resumes and filling out applications?"
- "What happens once you land the interview — do you have anything helping you in the room?"

### Proof Points
- Auto-Apply removes the manual submission step entirely
- Interview Copilot works live, during the real interview — Teal has nothing at this stage

## vs. Kickresume

### Their Pitch
"AI resume and cover letter builder with templates."

### Their Strengths
- Strong template library, good design polish
- Cover letter generation

### Their Weaknesses
- **Stops at the document** — no application automation, no interview support
- **Generic AI tailoring** — not matched line-by-line against the specific job description's ATS keywords
- **No practice or live coaching**

### Our Counter
"Kickresume makes a resume look great. Lightforth makes sure it actually gets past the ATS for THIS job, then goes with you all the way into the interview room."

### Key Questions
- "Once your resume looks good, what's helping you actually get responses?"
- "What happens in the interview itself?"

### Proof Points
- ATS-specific keyword matching per job description, not a generic template
- Interview Prep + Interview Copilot cover everything Kickresume doesn't touch

## vs. Jobscan

### Their Pitch
"ATS resume scanning and keyword optimization."

### Their Strengths
- Purpose-built ATS score, well-known in the space
- Keyword-gap analysis against a job description

### Their Weaknesses
- **Score-only** — tells you what's missing, doesn't rewrite it for you
- **No application, no interview support** — one narrow slice of the funnel
- **Pricing is comparable to Lightforth for a fraction of the feature set**

### Our Counter
"Jobscan tells you your resume scored 62%. Lightforth rewrites it to score 95% for you, then applies to the job, then preps you for the interview it gets you."

### Key Questions
- "After you get the score, who's actually fixing the resume?"
- "What are you using to prep for the interview once you land one?"

### Proof Points
- One platform covers scoring, tailoring, applying, prepping, and live coaching
- Comparable price to Jobscan alone, for the entire funnel

## vs. Big Interview / Pramp

### Their Pitch
"Mock interview practice with AI or peer partners."

### Their Strengths
- Structured practice question banks
- Peer-to-peer practice option (Pramp)

### Their Weaknesses
- **Practice only — nothing during the real interview**
- **No resume or application tooling** — a single-purpose tool
- **Peer practice quality varies** (Pramp)

### Our Counter
"Practice is good. But the real test is the actual interview — and that's exactly where Big Interview and Pramp stop helping. Copilot is with you live, in the room that matters."

### Key Questions
- "What's your plan for the actual interview, not just the practice round?"
- "How many mock sessions vs. real interviews have you done this month?"

### Proof Points
- Interview Prep covers the mock-practice use case AND Copilot covers the live interview — Big Interview/Pramp only do the former

## General Objection Handling

### "I'm already using a free tool"
"Totally fine to start free — the question is whether that free tool gets you through the ATS for THIS specific job, and whether it's with you once you're actually in the interview. Those are the two places most job searches actually stall."

### "It's too expensive"
"What's one job worth to you? If Premium gets you the offer two weeks sooner, it's already paid for itself. And it's month-to-month — no lock-in."

### "I need to think about it"
"Totally fair — the 14-day trial is free and doesn't need a card. Would it make sense to try it on your next real application instead of deciding in the abstract?"
