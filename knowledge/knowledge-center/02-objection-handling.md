# Lightforth — Objection Handling Guide

## Overview
Proven responses to the objections a rep actually hears from job seekers (and from coach/organization buyers). Use the L.A.R.C. framework: **Listen, Acknowledge, Respond, Confirm.**

---

## Price Objections

### "It's too expensive"
**Framework:** Reframe on cost-per-outcome, not monthly price.

1. **Acknowledge:** "I hear you — $79 a month is real money."
2. **Reframe:** "What's one job offer worth to you, though?"
3. **Quantify:** "If Premium gets you the offer even two weeks sooner, it's already paid for itself many times over."
4. **De-risk:** "And it's month-to-month — cancel anytime, no contract."

**Example:** "I hear you. But what's one job worth to you? If this gets you hired two weeks sooner, it's already paid for itself — and you're never locked in past a month."

### "I can't afford it right now"
**Framework:** Offer the real, immediate alternative.

1. **Acknowledge:** "Totally understand — money's tight during a search."
2. **Offer:** "Start with the free tier and Pro's 14-day trial — no card needed to try it."
3. **Reframe:** "If it helps you land something faster, it pays for itself out of your first paycheck."

### "Your competitor is cheaper"
**Framework:** Differentiate on what's actually included.

1. **Acknowledge:** "Price matters, I get it."
2. **Differentiate:** "What are they including — just a resume tool, or the whole funnel through the live interview?"
3. **Reframe:** "We're not the cheapest resume tool. We're the only one that's still with you once you're in the actual interview."

## "Isn't this cheating?" (the #1 objection unique to this product)

**Framework:** Answer directly, don't dodge.

1. **Acknowledge:** "That's the first thing most people ask, and it's a fair question."
2. **Reframe:** "Copilot doesn't put words in your mouth you don't mean. It surfaces the relevant point from your own resume and experience — fast, in the moment nerves make you blank."
3. **Normalize:** "Most people don't fail interviews because they don't know the answer. They fail because they freeze. This fixes the freeze, not the honesty."

**Example:** "Fair question. It's not feeding you a script — it's pulling from your own real experience, faster than nerves let you recall it yourself. You still have to actually be the person who did the work."

## "I can do this myself"

### "I can write my own resume"
1. **Acknowledge:** "You know your experience better than anyone."
2. **Reframe:** "The hard part isn't writing — it's re-matching the exact keywords an ATS is scanning for, for every single posting. That's the part that's genuinely tedious to do by hand 40 times."
3. **Ask:** "How many versions of your resume do you have right now?"

### "I don't need Auto-Apply, I'll apply myself"
1. **Acknowledge:** "Totally your call."
2. **Reframe:** "Most people using it aren't replacing their judgment — they're removing the hour of form-filling on jobs they've already decided to apply to."
3. **Reassure:** "And you approve every application before it goes out — nothing submits on its own."

## Timing Objections

### "I need to think about it"
1. **Acknowledge:** "Totally fair, it's your money."
2. **Uncover:** "What specifically do you want to think through?"
3. **Redirect:** "The 14-day trial is free, no card needed — would it make more sense to just try it on your next real application instead of deciding in the abstract?"

### "I'm not actively job searching yet"
1. **Understand:** "When are you planning to start?"
2. **Create relevance:** "A tailored resume takes 10 minutes now — worth having ready before you need it?"
3. **Offer:** "Start free, upgrade when you're actually applying."

---

## "I already tried a similar tool"

### "I tried [Teal / Kickresume / Jobscan] and it didn't work"
1. **Understand:** "What was missing?"
2. **Differentiate:** "Most of those stop at the resume or the score — they don't follow you into the actual live interview. That's usually the real gap."
3. **Offer proof:** "Would seeing Copilot live in a mock interview change your mind?"

## Authority / Buy-In Objections

### "I need to talk to my partner/family about the money"
1. **Acknowledge:** "Makes sense, that's a real household decision."
2. **Equip:** "Want me to send a one-pager on the plans and what's included so it's an easy conversation?"
3. **Propose:** "The 14-day trial doesn't need a card, so there's no financial decision yet if you want to try it together first."

## Bundle / Organization Objections (Coaches, Bootcamps, Career Centers)

### "My budget for tools is already committed this year"
1. **Understand:** "When does your budget reset?"
2. **Quantify:** "What's the cost of your placement rate staying flat vs. giving your cohort a tool that reinforces coaching between sessions?"
3. **Offer:** "Would a smaller pilot bundle (5-9 seats) make sense to test with one cohort first?"

### "Will this replace what I do as a coach?"
1. **Acknowledge:** "Great question — it's a fair concern."
2. **Reframe:** "It's not a replacement for your judgment and relationship with your students. It's what fills the gap between your sessions, when you can't personally run 30 mock interviews in the same week."

### "How do I know my students will actually use their seats?"
1. **Acknowledge:** "Fair — unused seats are wasted money."
2. **Offer:** "The admin view shows seat activation, so you can follow up with anyone who hasn't started. And unused seats can be reassigned anytime."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Don't dodge the "is this cheating" question** — answer it head-on, every time it comes up
- **Don't lead with price** — lead with the actual gap (ATS matching, live interview support), then land on price
- **Don't oversell Auto-Apply as "fully automatic"** — always mention the approval step, it builds trust
- **Don't forget to propose the trial as the next step**, not a vague "let me know"
