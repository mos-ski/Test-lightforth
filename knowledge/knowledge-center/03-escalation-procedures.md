# Lightforth — Escalation Procedures

## Overview
When and how to escalate during a sales or support conversation. Escalating fast is a tool to save the customer, not a sign of failure.

---

## When to Escalate

### Refund Requests Outside Policy
**Trigger:** Customer asks for a refund past the 14-day window, or wants an exception to standard policy
**Escalate to:** Support Lead
**Timeline:** Within 24 hours

### Discount Requests
**Trigger:** Any discount beyond what's self-serve (student/referral/annual)
**Escalate to:** Sales Lead (bundles under 20 seats) or Founder/CVO (20+ seats, or any exception request)
**Timeline:** 24-48 hours — see `knowledge-center/04-discount-guidelines.md`

### Live-Interview-Blocking Bug
**Trigger:** A customer reports Copilot failing to work *during or immediately before* a real scheduled interview
**Escalate to:** Support Lead, treat as urgent
**Timeline:** Immediately — this is the single highest-stakes support scenario in the product

### At-Risk / Upset Customer
**Trigger:** Customer expressing strong dissatisfaction or threatening to cancel and post publicly
**Escalate to:** Support Lead
**Timeline:** Within 4 hours

### Billing Disputes
**Trigger:** Customer disputes a charge or requests a refund outside standard policy
**Escalate to:** Support Lead + Finance
**Timeline:** Within 24 hours

### Bundle / Organization Issues
**Trigger:** Seat provisioning errors, an organization disputing invoiced seat counts, or a coach reporting low cohort adoption
**Escalate to:** Sales Lead
**Timeline:** Within 24 hours

---

## Escalation Process

### Step 1: Document the Situation
- Customer email/account
- What happened, in their words
- What's been tried so far
- Requested outcome

### Step 2: Choose the Right Path

| Situation | Escalate To | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Refund outside policy | Support Lead | 24 hours |
| Discount request | Sales Lead / Founder | 24-48 hours |
| Live-interview-blocking bug | Support Lead | Immediately |
| At-risk/upset customer | Support Lead | 4 hours |
| Billing dispute | Support Lead + Finance | 24 hours |
| Bundle/organization issue | Sales Lead | 24 hours |

### Step 3: Follow Up
- Update the customer with a clear timeline, even if the answer isn't final yet
- Document the outcome for future reference

## Escalation Email Template

```
Subject: Escalation: [Customer email] — [Brief issue]

Hi [Name],

Need a hand with this one:

Customer: [Email]
Issue: [What happened]
Tried so far: [Actions taken]
Requested outcome: [What they want]
Urgency: [Normal / Same-day / Immediate — live interview affected]

Can you take a look today?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

## Best Practices
- **Escalate live-interview-blocking issues immediately** — nothing else in this product is this time-sensitive
- **Document before escalating** — don't make someone re-ask the customer what already happened
- **Always follow up with the customer**, even just to say it's being looked at
- **Learn from it** — note the resolution so the same issue is faster to handle next time
