# Sales Copilot Test Scripts — Play the Skeptical Prospect

Use these to test the Sales Copilot after uploading the rewritten knowledge base. **You play the prospect** (read the lines out loud on a real or test call), your rep uses the Copilot, and you watch whether the suggested responses actually handle you well. Each scenario is designed to resist, not fold immediately — a good copilot should still be making progress by the last line.

**How to run it:** Have your rep open the desktop app on the Sales Call use case with a matching deal-stage/context set up for the scenario. Call them (or sit across from them) and read through a scenario's lines, pausing after each one for the rep to respond with (or without) Copilot's suggestion. Don't skip ahead — react to what they actually say, and only move to the next line once they've responded.

**What "good" looks like:** the suggested response should directly answer what you just said (not a generic pitch), reference something concrete (a specific feature, price, or proof point), and end with a question or clear next step — not just information.

---

## Scenario 1: The Skeptical Individual — Price + "Is This Even Real"

**Persona:** Mid-career professional, recently laid off, actively job searching, hasn't bought anything like this before. Price-sensitive and suspicious of AI hype.

**Objective for the rep:** Get you to agree to start the 14-day free trial today.

1. "Hi — yeah, I saw the ad. Honestly I'm not sure this is for me, I'm just trying to figure out what it actually does."
2. "$79 a month feels like a lot right now, I'm not even making money at the moment."
3. "I don't know... doesn't it feel a little like cheating to have an AI feeding you answers in an actual interview?"
4. "I've already been using ChatGPT to help with my resume for free — why do I need to pay for this?"
5. "I think I just want to think about it for a bit and come back later."
6. *(If they propose the trial)* "Does it need a credit card? I don't want to get charged without realizing it."

## Scenario 2: The Recent Grad ("Student") — Budget + "Does This Even Apply to Me"

**Persona:** About to graduate, zero real interview experience, very budget-constrained, unsure if a tool built around "sales-y" job hunting even applies to entry-level roles.

**Objective for the rep:** Get you onto the free tier/trial and pointed at the student discount.

1. "Hey, I'm about to graduate and I've literally never had a real interview before — I don't even know if this is for someone like me."
2. "I'm a student, I genuinely don't have $49 a month lying around."
3. "Does this actually work for internships and entry-level jobs, or is it more for people with actual careers already?"
4. "How do I know the AI won't say something totally wrong in the interview and make me look worse, not better?"
5. "Is there any kind of discount for students, or is it the same price for everyone?"
6. "I guess I could try the free trial, but I really don't want to get auto-charged after."

## Scenario 3: The Career Coach — Bundle Buyer, Protective of Their Own Role

**Persona:** Runs a small independent career-coaching practice or a coding bootcamp, ~15-20 people to place. Already does mock interviews personally. Worried the tool either doesn't help enough to justify the cost, or replaces what makes them valuable.

**Objective for the rep:** Get a bundle discussion moving toward a pilot (5-10 seats).

1. "So walk me through this — I already run mock interviews with my students myself. Why would I pay for this too?"
2. "I don't want my students thinking they don't need me anymore because they've got an app."
3. "Twenty seats at full price is a lot for a program my size — is there any kind of discount for smaller cohorts?"
4. "Will I actually be able to see what my students are doing in there, or am I just paying and hoping they use it?"
5. "Do you have any actual proof this moves placement numbers, or is that just a sales line?"
6. "Let me think about it and get back to you after this cohort graduates."

## Scenario 4: Burned-Out Free Trial User — Already Used the Credits, Unimpressed

**Persona:** Signed up, burned through the free credits already, wasn't impressed, and this call is a save/win-back attempt before they churn or never convert.

**Objective for the rep:** Re-engage you with something you *haven't* tried yet (usually Interview Copilot, since free credits rarely stretch to a live interview) and get you to upgrade.

1. "Yeah, I used the free credits already, honestly... I wasn't that impressed."
2. "The resume it gave me didn't feel that different from what I already had, if I'm being honest."
3. "I didn't even get an interview off the applications it helped me send, so I don't know if it's actually worth paying for."
4. "Why would I pay $49 now when the free version didn't really wow me?"
5. "Maybe if you can show me something I haven't already seen, I'd reconsider — but I'm skeptical."
6. *(If they pivot to Interview Copilot specifically)* "Wait, I didn't realize the free credits didn't even include that — how does that actually work, live, in a real interview?"

---

## After Each Test Run — What to Note
- Did the suggested response answer the *specific* thing you said, or a generic version of the objection?
- Did it reference real facts (correct price, correct discount %, a real competitor) or something made up?
- Did it end in a clear next step, or just trail off?
- Which scenario broke down first — that's where the knowledge base likely needs another pass.
